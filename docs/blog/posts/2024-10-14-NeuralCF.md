# Neural Collaborative Filtering

In this post well cover some of the basics of recommendation system approaches utilising neural networks.

We covered collaborative filtration using matrix factorisation is the [notebook](https://shtrausslearning.github.io/notebooks/course_recsys/recsys_matrix_decomposition)

Collaborative filtering (CF) is a popular recommendation technique that relies on user-item interactions to make predictions about what a user might like based on the preferences of similar users or items. There are two main types of collaborative filtering: memory-based and model-based.

▎Traditional Collaborative Filtering


2. Model-Based Collaborative Filtering:

   • This approach builds a model based on the user-item interaction data. Techniques include matrix factorization methods (like Singular Value Decomposition or SVD), clustering, and Bayesian networks.

▎Neural Collaborative Filtering

Neural Collaborative Filtering (NCF) is an advanced version of model-based collaborative filtering that leverages deep learning techniques to enhance recommendation performance. Here are some key differences:

1. Architecture:

   • NCF employs neural networks to learn complex patterns in user-item interactions, whereas traditional CF often relies on simpler linear models or matrix factorization techniques.

2. Representation Learning:

   • NCF can learn dense embeddings for users and items through neural networks, capturing non-linear relationships and interactions between users and items, which traditional methods may miss.

3. Flexibility:

   • NCF can easily incorporate additional features, such as user demographics or item attributes, making it more flexible than traditional CF methods, which typically focus solely on user-item interaction data.

4. Scalability:

   • Neural networks can handle large datasets more efficiently than some traditional methods, especially when leveraging techniques like mini-batch training and GPU acceleration.

5. Performance:

   • In many cases, NCF has been shown to outperform traditional collaborative filtering methods in terms of recommendation accuracy, especially in scenarios with complex user-item interactions.

▎Summary

In summary, while traditional collaborative filtering relies on simpler statistical techniques to find similarities in user preferences or item characteristics, neural collaborative filtering uses deep learning models to capture complex patterns and relationships in the data, often resulting in improved performance and flexibility.
